Speaking & Thought Leadership

Sharing hard-won lessons from leading enterprise programs at scale

Ronbo Fan is passionate about sharing practical insights with the broader technology community. His talks draw from real-world experience managing multi-million-dollar cloud infrastructure programs, leading agile transformations across large engineering organizations, and navigating the complexities of enterprise virtualization at Citrix. He focuses on actionable frameworks and honest lessons — not theory divorced from practice. For a deeper look at the experience behind these talks, see Professional Experience and Insights.

Speaking Topics

Scaling Cloud Infrastructure for the Enterprise

This talk explores the architectural patterns, operational practices, and program management strategies required to scale cloud infrastructure from serving thousands of users to millions. Drawing from his work at Citrix, Ronbo walks through the real challenges of hybrid multi-cloud deployments — including capacity planning across AWS and Azure, managing infrastructure-as-code at scale with Terraform, designing for 99.9%+ availability, and coordinating infrastructure programs across globally distributed engineering teams. Attendees learn how to structure infrastructure scaling programs with clear milestones, how to build observability into the scaling process using tools like Datadog and Grafana, and how to communicate infrastructure risk to non-technical stakeholders without losing fidelity. The talk includes a detailed case study of a capacity scaling initiative that supported a 3x increase in concurrent virtual desktop sessions within a single quarter.

The Future of Desktop Virtualization: DaaS, VDI, and Beyond

Desktop virtualization is undergoing a fundamental transformation as enterprises shift from traditional on-premises VDI to cloud-native Desktop-as-a-Service models. In this talk, Ronbo examines the technical and organizational implications of this shift, covering session management architectures, image lifecycle optimization, secure remote delivery protocols, and the evolving role of edge computing in workspace delivery. He discusses the trade-offs between persistent and non-persistent desktops, the impact of GPU virtualization on user experience, and how DaaS platforms are adapting to support emerging workloads like AI-assisted development environments. The talk also addresses the program management challenges unique to platform migrations — managing customer expectations during transition periods, coordinating feature parity across legacy and next-generation systems, and building rollback strategies for enterprise-grade reliability.

Agile at Scale: Implementing SAFe in Large Engineering Organizations

Implementing the Scaled Agile Framework in a large engineering organization is one of the most complex transformations a technology company can undertake. This talk provides a practitioner's perspective on what actually works, what commonly fails, and how to navigate the organizational politics that often derail agile transformations. Ronbo shares his experience designing and launching Agile Release Trains, structuring effective PI planning ceremonies for 100+ engineers, establishing program-level Kanban systems for dependency management, and measuring agile maturity with metrics that actually matter. He covers the critical role of the Technical Program Manager in SAFe implementations — serving as the connective tissue between product management, engineering, and executive leadership. The talk includes specific anti-patterns he has encountered, such as "SAFe theater" where ceremonies exist without substance, and practical techniques for building genuine agile culture rather than superficial process compliance.

The TPM's Guide to Cross-Functional Leadership

Technical Program Managers lead without direct authority, making cross-functional leadership one of the most essential — and most misunderstood — skills in the TPM toolkit. This talk breaks down the specific techniques Ronbo uses to align engineering, product management, design, QA, DevOps, and executive stakeholders around shared program objectives. He covers how to build influence through radical transparency, how to structure communication for different audiences (from individual contributors to C-suite executives), and how to navigate organizational ambiguity when priorities conflict. The presentation includes frameworks for running effective program reviews, escalation strategies that preserve relationships, and techniques for building trust across teams that may have competing incentives. Ronbo also addresses the career development dimension, discussing how aspiring TPMs can develop these leadership skills intentionally rather than accidentally.

Release Engineering and Zero-Downtime Deployments

Shipping enterprise software with zero downtime requires a sophisticated orchestration of CI/CD pipelines, deployment strategies, and cross-team coordination. In this talk, Ronbo dives into the release engineering practices that enable Citrix to deploy platform updates to millions of enterprise users without service interruption. He covers blue-green and canary deployment patterns, feature flag management at scale, automated rollback mechanisms, and the program management frameworks that coordinate release activities across multiple scrum teams and time zones. The talk addresses how to define meaningful release gating criteria, how to build confidence in automated testing suites, and how to structure blameless post-incident reviews that drive genuine improvement. Attendees leave with a practical playbook for building a release engineering culture that balances velocity with reliability — shipping fast without breaking things.
