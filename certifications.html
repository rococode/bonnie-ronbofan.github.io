Certifications & Continuous Learning

Industry credentials and an ongoing commitment to professional growth

Professional Certifications

Each certification below represents a deliberate investment in deepening domain expertise. Ronbo Fan pursues credentials that directly reinforce his ability to lead complex technical programs, and he actively applies the frameworks and methodologies they represent in his day-to-day work at Citrix.

SAFe 5 Program Consultant (SPC)

Scaled Agile, Inc. — 2023

The SAFe Program Consultant certification is the highest-impact credential in the Scaled Agile Framework ecosystem, qualifying holders to train and coach teams, launch Agile Release Trains, and drive enterprise-wide Lean-Agile transformation. Ronbo earned this certification to formalize his experience implementing SAFe across large engineering organizations. At Citrix, he applies SPC principles to design PI planning ceremonies for 100+ engineers, establish program-level Kanban systems, and coach scrum masters on continuous delivery practices that have measurably reduced cycle times.

AWS Solutions Architect — Associate

Amazon Web Services — 2022

The AWS Solutions Architect Associate certification validates the ability to design distributed systems on AWS, covering compute, storage, networking, databases, and security services. Ronbo pursued this credential to strengthen his technical fluency when leading cloud infrastructure programs. He leverages this knowledge daily when reviewing architecture decisions for Citrix's hybrid multi-cloud deployments, evaluating capacity planning proposals, and ensuring that infrastructure programs align with AWS best practices for cost optimization, high availability, and disaster recovery.

Professional Scrum Master (PSM II)

Scrum.org — 2021

PSM II is an advanced Scrum mastery certification that goes beyond basic framework mechanics to assess a practitioner's ability to apply Scrum in complex, real-world situations. It tests deep understanding of Scrum theory, servant leadership, coaching techniques, and organizational impediment resolution. Ronbo uses his PSM II knowledge to mentor scrum teams, facilitate difficult retrospectives, and design custom agile workflows that respect Scrum's core principles while adapting to the operational realities of enterprise software delivery.

Citrix Certified Professional — Virtualization (CCP-V)

Citrix — 2020

The CCP-V certification demonstrates advanced expertise in Citrix virtualization technologies, including Citrix Virtual Apps and Desktops, provisioning services, and workspace environment management. As a TPM embedded in Citrix's DaaS organization, Ronbo earned this certification to deepen his understanding of the product architecture he manages programs for. This credential enables him to speak the language of his engineering teams with precision, evaluate technical trade-offs in design reviews, and ensure that program milestones align with the platform's architectural constraints and capabilities.

ITIL 4 Foundation

Axelos — 2019

ITIL 4 Foundation provides a comprehensive understanding of IT service management best practices, covering service value systems, guiding principles, and the four dimensions of service management. Ronbo applies ITIL concepts to structure incident management workflows, define service level objectives for the platforms his programs support, and ensure that release management processes follow established change management protocols. This framework is particularly valuable when coordinating between development teams and operations during production deployments.

PMP (Project Management Professional)

Project Management Institute (PMI) — 2018

The PMP certification is the gold standard in project management, validating expertise in predictive, agile, and hybrid project management approaches. It covers scope, schedule, cost, quality, risk, procurement, and stakeholder management across the full project lifecycle. Ronbo earned his PMP early in his career as a foundational credential, and its principles continue to inform how he structures program charters, manages budgets, negotiates with vendors, and builds risk registers for multi-quarter enterprise initiatives. The PMP's emphasis on earned value management and quantitative analysis complements his more agile-focused certifications.

Continuous Learning

Ronbo Fan believes that professional certifications are milestones, not destinations. The technology landscape evolves rapidly, and the skills required to lead enterprise programs today are meaningfully different from those needed even two years ago. To stay at the forefront, Ronbo maintains a disciplined approach to continuous learning that spans multiple channels and formats.

He dedicates several hours each week to reading technical publications, engineering blogs from companies like Netflix, Google, and Amazon, and research papers on distributed systems and cloud-native architectures. He is an active participant in online communities focused on technical program management, site reliability engineering, and platform engineering, where he both learns from and contributes to peer discussions on emerging best practices.

Ronbo also invests in structured learning through online courses on platforms such as Coursera, LinkedIn Learning, and A Cloud Guru, focusing on areas like advanced Kubernetes orchestration, observability engineering, and machine learning infrastructure — domains that are increasingly relevant to the programs he manages. He views this ongoing investment not as optional professional development, but as a core responsibility of any technical leader who needs to make informed decisions about complex engineering systems.

Recommended Learning Paths

For aspiring Technical Program Managers looking to build a similar foundation, Ronbo recommends the following progression:

Foundation (Years 1-2)

Start with PMP or CAPM to build project management fundamentals. Simultaneously, pursue Scrum.org's PSM I to understand agile delivery mechanics. Complement these with hands-on exposure to JIRA, Confluence, and basic CI/CD concepts. Read The Phoenix Project and The Manager's Path to build mental models for how engineering organizations operate.

Intermediate (Years 2-4)

Advance to PSM II and begin pursuing a cloud certification (AWS Solutions Architect Associate or Azure Fundamentals). Study ITIL 4 Foundation to understand service management. Start attending industry conferences and contributing to TPM communities. Read Accelerate and Team Topologies to deepen understanding of engineering effectiveness.

Advanced (Years 4+)

Pursue SAFe SPC if working in scaled agile environments. Earn domain-specific certifications relevant to your industry (e.g., CCP-V for virtualization, CKA for Kubernetes). Focus on executive communication skills and strategic thinking. Consider An Elegant Puzzle and SAFe 5.0 Distilled for leadership and framework mastery. Explore speaking opportunities to build thought leadership.
