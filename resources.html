Resources for Technical Program Managers

Books, tools, frameworks, and communities that have shaped how Ronbo approaches the craft

The resources below represent Ronbo Fan's personal recommendations — tools, books, and communities that have directly influenced how he manages programs at Citrix. This is not an exhaustive list, but a curated selection of the highest-signal resources for Technical Program Managers at any stage of their career. For related content, see Technical Skills, Certifications, and Insights.

Recommended Books

These books have had the greatest impact on how Ronbo thinks about engineering organizations, delivery systems, and technical leadership. Each is recommended for a specific reason.

The Phoenix Project

Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford

A novel about IT operations and DevOps that reads like a thriller. It illustrates the Three Ways — systems thinking, amplifying feedback loops, and continuous experimentation — through a fictional company's transformation. Essential reading for any TPM who wants to understand why delivery pipelines break and how to fix them. Ronbo considers this the single best introduction to the cultural and operational dynamics of modern software delivery.

Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps

Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim

The research-backed companion to The Phoenix Project. Accelerate identifies the specific capabilities — continuous delivery, trunk-based development, loosely coupled architecture, and others — that predict high-performing engineering organizations. Ronbo uses the DORA metrics framework from this book to measure program health and advocate for engineering investments with data rather than intuition.

Team Topologies

Matthew Skelton, Manuel Pais

A practical guide to organizing engineering teams for fast flow. The book introduces four fundamental team types — stream-aligned, enabling, complicated-subsystem, and platform — and three interaction modes. Ronbo references Team Topologies frequently when designing program structures and advocating for organizational changes that reduce cognitive load and improve delivery velocity across cross-functional teams.

An Elegant Puzzle: Systems of Engineering Management

Will Larson

A systems-thinking approach to engineering management that covers organizational design, hiring, technical debt, and strategic planning. While written for engineering managers, the frameworks are directly applicable to TPMs — particularly the sections on sizing teams, managing technical migrations, and presenting to executives. Ronbo finds the chapter on "systems thinking for organizational change" especially relevant to large-scale program management.

The Manager's Path

Camille Fournier

A guide to technical management from tech lead through CTO. For TPMs, this book provides invaluable insight into how engineering managers think, what they worry about, and how they make decisions — knowledge that is critical for building effective cross-functional partnerships. The chapters on managing multiple teams and navigating organizational politics are particularly relevant to the TPM role.

Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love

Marty Cagan

The definitive guide to modern product management. Understanding how product managers think is essential for TPMs who need to translate product vision into executable engineering programs. Cagan's frameworks for product discovery, dual-track agile, and empowered product teams inform how Ronbo structures the interface between product management and engineering delivery in his programs.

SAFe 5.0 Distilled

Richard Knaster, Dean Leffingwell

The most accessible entry point to the Scaled Agile Framework. This book distills the core principles, practices, and roles of SAFe into a concise format that is practical without being overwhelming. Ronbo recommends it as a companion to formal SAFe certification training, and as a reference text for teams beginning their scaled agile journey. It is especially useful for understanding the Agile Release Train construct and PI planning mechanics.

Essential Tools & Platforms

These are the tools Ronbo uses daily or recommends for TPMs managing enterprise-scale programs.

JIRA

The industry-standard work management platform for agile teams. Ronbo uses JIRA for sprint planning, backlog management, cross-team dependency tracking, and program-level reporting. Mastering JIRA's advanced features — custom workflows, automation rules, dashboards, and JQL queries — is a non-negotiable skill for any TPM working in an agile environment.

Confluence

Atlassian's documentation and collaboration platform. Ronbo uses Confluence as the single source of truth for program documentation — including program charters, decision logs, architecture reviews, and PI planning artifacts. Effective use of Confluence reduces meeting overhead and ensures that program context is accessible to any stakeholder at any time.

Datadog

A cloud-scale monitoring and observability platform. TPMs who manage infrastructure programs need to understand system health metrics, and Datadog provides the dashboards, alerts, and APM traces that make this possible. Ronbo uses Datadog to monitor production health during releases and to build executive-facing dashboards that translate infrastructure metrics into business language.

Terraform

HashiCorp's infrastructure-as-code tool for provisioning and managing cloud resources. While TPMs do not typically write Terraform configurations, understanding IaC concepts is essential for managing infrastructure programs effectively. Ronbo's familiarity with Terraform enables him to participate meaningfully in architecture reviews, evaluate infrastructure change proposals, and understand deployment dependencies.

Grafana & Prometheus

Open-source monitoring and alerting tools widely used in cloud-native environments. Grafana's visualization capabilities and Prometheus's metrics collection provide deep visibility into system performance. Ronbo uses these tools to track SLIs and SLOs for the platforms his programs support, and to build data-driven narratives for capacity planning proposals.

Git & GitHub

Version control and collaboration platform. TPMs benefit from understanding Git workflows — branching strategies, pull request processes, and CI/CD integration — because these directly impact release cadence and team velocity. Ronbo uses GitHub to track engineering activity, review release notes, and understand the technical scope of program deliverables.

Frameworks & Methodologies

Frameworks provide the scaffolding for consistent, repeatable program execution. These are the methodologies Ronbo applies most frequently.

SAFe (Scaled Agile Framework)

The most widely adopted framework for scaling agile practices across large enterprises. SAFe provides structures for Agile Release Trains, PI Planning, program-level Kanban, and Lean Portfolio Management. Ronbo has implemented SAFe at the program level for teams of 100+ engineers and holds the SAFe Program Consultant (SPC) certification. For more on his SAFe experience, see Certifications.

Scrum

The foundational agile framework for iterative software delivery. Scrum's sprint-based cadence, defined roles (Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team), and ceremonies (sprint planning, daily standup, retrospective) form the backbone of delivery at most engineering organizations. Ronbo coordinates across multiple Scrum teams simultaneously, aligning sprint goals with program-level milestones.

OKRs (Objectives and Key Results)

A goal-setting framework that aligns individual, team, and organizational objectives with measurable outcomes. Ronbo uses OKRs to connect program deliverables to business outcomes, ensuring that every engineering initiative can be traced back to a strategic objective. He finds OKRs particularly valuable for facilitating quarterly planning discussions with executive stakeholders.

RICE Prioritization

A scoring framework that evaluates initiatives based on Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort. Ronbo uses RICE when collaborating with product managers on roadmap prioritization, providing a data-driven approach to sequencing work when resources are constrained and competing priorities are abundant. It brings objectivity to conversations that might otherwise be driven by organizational politics.

DORA Metrics

The four key metrics identified by the DevOps Research and Assessment team — deployment frequency, lead time for changes, change failure rate, and time to restore service. Ronbo tracks DORA metrics to assess engineering delivery health, identify bottlenecks, and build evidence-based cases for infrastructure and process investments.

Communities & Newsletters

Staying connected to the broader TPM and engineering leadership community is essential for continuous growth. These are the communities and publications Ronbo finds most valuable.

The Pragmatic Engineer Newsletter

by Gergely Orosz

One of the most widely read newsletters in the software engineering leadership space. Gergely covers topics ranging from engineering management and technical strategy to industry trends and compensation. The newsletter frequently addresses TPM-relevant topics and provides insider perspectives on how top technology companies operate.

Lenny's Newsletter

by Lenny Rachitsky

A product management focused newsletter that provides deep-dives into product strategy, growth frameworks, and organizational design. While product-focused, the content is highly relevant to TPMs who work closely with product teams and need to understand how product decisions translate into engineering programs.

Rands Leadership Slack

A vibrant Slack community of engineering leaders, managers, and program managers. Channels dedicated to program management, agile practices, and career development provide a space for real-time peer discussions. Ronbo has found this community invaluable for pressure-testing ideas, seeking advice on difficult program challenges, and staying connected to how other organizations approach similar problems.

TPM Community on LinkedIn

Several active LinkedIn groups and communities focused specifically on Technical Program Management, including "Technical Program Managers" and "TPM Network." These groups share job postings, career advice, and discussions on TPM-specific challenges like dependency management, stakeholder alignment, and cross-functional leadership at scale.

SRE Weekly

by Lex Neva

A curated newsletter covering site reliability engineering, incident response, and operational excellence. For TPMs managing infrastructure programs, understanding SRE practices and recent outage analyses is critical context for making informed decisions about reliability investments and release risk management.

LeadDev

A media platform offering articles, conferences, and community content for engineering leaders. LeadDev's content on managing engineering teams, building technical strategy, and navigating organizational dynamics is directly applicable to the TPM role. Their annual conferences feature talks from practitioners at companies like Google, Stripe, and Shopify.
